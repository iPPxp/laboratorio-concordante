Laboratorio Concordante
Towards an Engineering of Explanatory Models
Concept Note for Discussion
Abstract
The Laboratorio Concordante is proposed as a formal research program devoted to the construction, evaluation, comparison, reduction and evolution of explanatory models. Rather than asking whether a theory is true, it studies the conditions under which an explanatory model justifies continuing to exist in competition with alternatives. The proposal seeks to transform methodological criteria into explicit, auditable and reproducible procedures.
1. Motivation
Modern science possesses many criteria for evaluating theories—simplicity, explanatory power, coherence, predictive success and falsifiability—but these are typically applied informally and independently. The project asks whether these criteria can be integrated into a unified formal architecture that supports systematic auditing of explanatory models.
2. Central Hypothesis
A methodology can itself be represented as a formal system composed of axioms, operators and protocols. Such a system should remain permanently auditable and explicitly incomplete, preventing methodological dogmatism.
3. Proposed Architecture
• Canon: operational principles governing admissibility.
• Formal operators (e.g., Ξ) for conceptual reduction.
• Explicit protocols for admission, reduction, validation and demolition.
• Mathematical foundations supporting proofs about the operators.
• Continuous self-auditing of the methodology.
4. Distinctive Features
The proposal is not a substantive scientific theory. It is methodological infrastructure intended to evaluate scientific, philosophical or computational explanatory models. The emphasis is on reproducible procedures rather than intuitive judgment.
5. Research Questions
1. Can explanatory equivalence be defined formally?
2. Which concepts are irreducible within explanatory systems?
3. Can methodological auditing itself be formalized?
4. What mathematical guarantees can be proven for reduction operators?
6. Relationship with Existing Work
The project is intended to complement—not replace—work in philosophy of science, formal epistemology and metatheory. It invites comparison with Popper, Kuhn, Lakatos and the structuralist program while pursuing a more explicitly operational and algorithmic perspective.
7. Current Status
The Canon, preliminary operators, protocols and an initial mathematical framework are under active development. The project is presented as an open research program whose own principles remain subject to revision.
Invitation
The purpose of this document is to invite critical evaluation. The primary questions are whether the program is conceptually coherent, whether comparable work already exists, and which areas require further formalization.
